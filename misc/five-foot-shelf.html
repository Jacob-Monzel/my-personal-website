The Five-Foot Shelf of Books Hunt

The Quest

In 1909, Charles W. Eliot, president of Harvard, claimed a liberal education could be had by reading fifteen minutes a day from a shelf of books five feet long. To this end, he published that exact shelf: the 51-volume Harvard Classics. Collier reprinted them for decades in several bindings. I am collecting the blue “Deluxe” printing (gilt spine, blind-stamped Veritas shield on the cover), plus its (maybe) rarer 20-volume companion, the Shelf of Fiction.

Rules of the hunt:

  • Physical stores only. No online orders, ever (at least for now).
  • Any blue Collier Deluxe printing counts — printing years don’t have to match. If it looks right on the shelf, it’s right.
  • No time limit.

The hunt began in June 2026.
