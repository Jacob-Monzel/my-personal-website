Books I've Read Since June 2025

I started tracking (non-math) books I have read in June 2025. I don’t include exact dates because I’m too lazy.
Ratings are 0–10 in 0.5 increments, "normally distributed" around 5.
  • 5 = average, maybe recommendable
  • 6 = pretty decent, a little below good
  • 7 = solidly good
  • 8 = great
  • 9 = amazing
  • 10 = life-defining favorite
  • Less than 5 = the opposite opinions of the above
  • Most stuff I read will be above 5 since I pick books that actually interest me and I refuse to finish books that bore me (one day I will add a DNF tag).
What We Owe The Future
In Progress
All Quiet on the Western Front
Rating: 9/10

Very engaging book. Very distubring as well. I had seen the 2022 movie before and assumed it would be like that but it was very different (in a better way). I assumed Paul would die at the end since that happens in the movie so I really did not want to even finish the book to avoid it. Very painful read altogether but in a "good" way.

Will I reread it? Yes (and the "sequal" book too)

Er kann mich im Arsche lecken

A Canticle for Leibowitz
Rating: 9/10

Probably the best book I have ever read (to this point) from the POV of enjoyment and well-writtenness. I have also assumed MAD would prevent a true nuclear war, but this book has made me think again about the nature of people and leaders.

Will I reread it? If I ever learn latin 100%. If I don't, 75% chance.

Fiat Voluntas Tua

Best to avoid being a Thon Taddeo type person

How High We Go in the Dark
Rating: 7/10

A much different book than I thought. It’s about a plague, yet deeply grounded in each character. Very surprising and neat how everything connects.

Will I reread it? Probably not, but it is still a good book.

East of Eden
In Progress
Blindsight
Rating: 9/10 (raised from 7/10 on reflection)

Really cool premise. The vampire stuff is even cooler. Raises fascinating ethical and philosophical questions — but some parts felt like word salad.

Will I reread it? Maybe the sequel someday. Would absolutely watch a movie.

Chinese Room — is consciousness a maladaptive trait?

The Forever War
Rating: 7.5/10

Not a masterpiece, but very enjoyable. Time dilation as a Vietnam allegory was brilliant. I’ve never fought in a war, but prisoners might relate to this book.

Will I reread it? Probably at least the sequels or a movie adaptation.

Everyone should find their Mary Gay in life.

The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August
Rating: 9/10

Really gripping. Great mix of pop science and philosophy. Raises cool questions. Being a kalachakra would probably suck — I’d want to forget everything eventually.

Will I reread it? Yeah… probably when I forget it in my next life.

Notes from a Dead House
Rating: 6.5/10

Dragged at times, but worth finishing. Dostoevsky’s Siberia/prison scenes are fascinating, just very far from my world. A good book, but felt like a chore.

Will I reread it? Maybe in many years.

This took forever to finish — “classics” often have boring stretches.

“Don’t let your mouth lust for another man’s crust…”

A Bend in the River
Rating: 5.5/10

Started decent, got weirder and worse. The sex stuff was off-putting. Feels like a post-colonial Things Fall Apart but less good. Good ambiguous ending though.

Will I reread it? Probably not.
